Coal Plants, Refineries and Cancer in the Continental United States

About This Visualization

In this visualization, you can look at Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma rates or overall cancer rates in counties for which data is published in the United States. Overlaid on that are power plants over 1000 MW whose primary fuel source is coal, as well as petroleum refineries. I chose this data because petroleum refineries produce benzene, and studies have found benzene exposure to increase Non-Hodgin Lymphoma rates. There are an unfortunate number of counties without published data for Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma rates, so it is hard to tell if there are any significant trends. However, note that in Louisiana, where there is an especially high density of petroleum refineries, there is also high rates of Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma.
