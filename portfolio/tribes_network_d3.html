Gahuku-Gama Sub-Tribe Relationship Network

About This Visualization

In a 1954 paper entitled Cultures of the Central Highlands, New Guinea, K. E. Read included a diagram of 16 Gahuku-Gama sub-tribes in the region, and their political ties and oppositions. While it was a reasonable diagram, I thought that now, more than 53 years later, it could use a colorful interactive update. In this interactive Network D3 visualization, the nodes are sized by betweenness, the green edges represent political ties, and the red edges represent opposition.

References and Credits

You can find the edge list here, the node list here, and the R code for making the visualization here. The research for the network came from the 1954 paper by K. E. Read entitled Cultures of the Central Highlands, New Guinea, and was published in the Southwestern Journal of Anthropology, Vol. 10, No. 1 (Spring, 1954), pp. 1-43. I downloaded the edge list from networkrepository.com, and manually determined which numbered nodes corresponded to which tribe names like a game of Sudoku.
