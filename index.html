Data Visualizations

  • U.S. Presidential Elections 1996-2016

    This visualization shows the evolving maps of state winners in the U.S. presidential general elections from 1996-2016. I built this multi-map visualization using Plotly.

  • Electricity Generation Sources Over Time

    This stacked barplot visualization shows how the mix of sources with which the United States generates electricity has evolved over time. I built this visualization using Plotly.

  • Cancer Incidence and Mortality in the United States

    This compound visualization, built with ggplot2, shows the breakdown of cancer rates by year and gender from 1999-2014, 2014 cancer rates by gender and race, and 2014 cancer rates by gender and leading site.

  • Capital Bikeshare Hourly Usage

    This time series visualization, built with Plotly, shows hourly trip counts over the course of three different weeks. You can use a slider and other tools to zoom in on different portions of time.

  • Capital Bikeshare Resident vs. Tourist Riders

    This set of visualizations, built with ggplot2, shows how resident vs. tourist usage of Capital Bikeshare differs. The visualizations use data from September 2016 and consider the number of trips taken each day for the two groups, the average trip lenth each day, and the most frequent stations for starting trips for the two groups.

  • The Link Between Poverty and Education

    This Tableau story shows the link between poverty and education in the United States. In the U.S. states, there is a strong positive correlation between rates of adults without a high school diploma and poverty rates. This visualization also shows the disparity between education and poverty levels in the North and South.

  • Trump's Twitter Associations

    This interactive Bokeh visualization shows the Twitter accounts (people and organizations) that Trump associates with each other, by analyzing and visualizing the tweets in which Trump mentions at least two Twitter accounts.

  • World Happiness Exploration

    This Tableau dashboard shows happiness levels around the world, lets you explore which countries are the happiest and least happy, and see which factors are most correlated with happiness levels.

  • Gahuku-Gama Sub-Tribe Relationship Network

    This interactive NetworkD3 visualization shows the relationships between the 16 Gahuku-Gama sub-tribes of the central highlands of New Guinea. You can click and drag the nodes to help highlight certain relationships.

  • Squirrel Wordcloud

    This silly squirrel wordcloud shows the words most frequently used in a non-scientific squirrel blog. I hope it makes you smile!

  • Coal Plants, Refineries and Cancer

    This choropleth map visualization, built with Leaflet, shows cancer rates or Non Hodgkin Lymphoma cancer rates by county, along with information about petroleum refineries and large coal-fired power plants.

About Me

I am a newly minted data scientist, and hope to use my skills to help solve environmental and social justice problems, and elect progressive political candidates.

Following a seven year career primarily focused on environmental advocacy, I decided to expand my advocacy and analysis toolkit via Georgetown's M.S. in data science program, and will graduate in May 2018.

My courses have covered topics including data visualization, natural language processing, algorithm design, optimization, longitudinal analysis, and Bayesian statistics. For collecting, cleaning, analyzing and visualizing data, my programming languages of choice are Python and R.

Outside of data science, I enjoy riding bicycles, hiking, cooking, playing the flute, and hanging out with my family's pet emu in the Hudson Valley.
