Global Terrorism Visualizations

These visualizations examine the types, locations, timing, and motivations of terrorism incidents to paint a picture of global terrorism and how it has evolved since 1970. Following a dip in terrorism incidents in the late 1990s and early 2000s, there has been a dramatic increase since 2004, particularly in the South Asia region (includes Afghanistan) and Middle East & North Africa region (includes Iraq). Also, since 1995, bombings, explosions and armed assaults have been the primary methods employed in terrorism incidents, while there was more of a mix in the 1970s including more assassinations and facility/infrastructure attacks. Please explore the visualizations below to learn about these trends and others, including taking a closer look at terrorism incidents in the United States.

An Increase in Suicide Terrorism

Along with the dramatic increase in terrorism, there has been an increase in suicide attacks. Through 1997, there were almost no suicide attacks; in 2016, 982 terrorism incidents (more than 7 percent of terrorism incidents) were suicides. Nikita Lalwani and Sam Winter-Levy discuss the reasons for this increase in a Washington Post article from August 16, 2016.

The Evolution of Terrorism in Iraq: 4 Snapshots

This visualization shows terrorism incidents in Iraq in 1976, 1996, 2006, and 2016, as relatively representative snapshots of these decades. There are no terrorism incidents listed in 1986 in Iraq (and not many generally in the 1980s) in the Global Terrorism Database. The number of incidents in Iraq has increased dramatically, with 3 in 1976, 0 in 1986, 832 in 2006, and 3356 in 2016.
